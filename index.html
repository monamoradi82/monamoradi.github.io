About Me

I am an AI Engineer specializing in Machine Learning, Edge AI, and IoT for digital healthcare. My work focuses on developing real-time, on-device intelligence for medical applications, improving latency, security, and scalability across distributed healthcare environments.

Currently, I am involved in designing AI-driven healthcare monitoring systems and secure IoT architectures, while actively contributing to European research and innovation initiatives. I have recently designed and developed an AI-powered mobile health application within the Proximity Care and Tuscany Health Ecosystem (THE) projects, integrating Edge AI, IoT connectivity, and voice-based risk detection.

With over 10 years of experience across academia and industry, I bring a strong background in computer engineering, computer architecture, and hardware design, including VLSI and nanoscale technologies. I am particularly interested in working at the edge of emerging technologies and bridging the gap between advanced research and real-world applications.

Beyond research, I enjoy exploring new places and cultures. I also have a strong interest in historical and novel literature, especially in history and philosophy, where I continuously seek new perspectives and insights.

Education

Ph.D. in Computer Engineering (Computer Architecture)

Institution: Faculty of Computer and Electrical Engineering, Islamic Azad University, Science and Research Branch, Tehran, Iran

Duration: February 2011 – September 2015

Thesis: Designing Computational Circuits Based on Current Mode Multi-Valued Logic Using CNTFET Technology

Abstract: This research focused on the design of multi-valued logic circuits using CNTFET technology to improve computational performance, reduce transistor count, and enhance efficiency in VLSI systems. Key contributions include novel ternary multipliers, decoders, and full-adder circuits, validated through Hspice 32nm simulations.

Funding: Supported by the Presidential Nanotechnology Initiative, Iran, inspired by the National Nanotechnology Initiative (USA), aiming to develop national nanotechnology priorities and facilitate R&D in both academic and industrial sectors.

Master of Science in Computer Engineering (Computer Architecture)

Institution: Faculty of Computer and Electrical Engineering, Islamic Azad University, Science and Research Branch, Tehran, Iran

Duration: September 2009 – September 2011

Thesis: Advanced Single-Bit Full Adder Designs Using CNTFET Technology

Abstract: Focused on designing and evaluating single-bit full adder circuits with CNTFET and MOSFET technologies. Proposed CNTFET designs demonstrated improved speed, power efficiency, and reduced area compared to conventional MOSFET-based circuits.

Funding: Supported by the Presidential Nanotechnology Initiative, Iran, following national strategies for advanced nanotechnology research, with guidance from governmental and private sectors.
